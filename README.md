# Document Control

The goal of doument control is to approve, reviwe, distribute and obsolete documents without losing tracability

## ISO 13485:2018

Documents that need to be tracked:

- Documented statements of a quality policy and quality objectives, what does the organization want to achieve with the QMS
- Quality manual, the blueprint of the QMS
- Documented procedures and records, procedures that describe how the QMS should operate
- Documents necessary to ensure effective planning, operation and control of its proceses
- Technical files of devices or medical device family (MDR/IVDR) equivalent to DHF and DMR (FDA)  

For each document the following requirement need to be met:

- Identify the current review state and changes.
- Documents shall remain legible and easily identifiable.
- The current version shall be available in the points of use.
- Prevent the non intended use of obsolete docuemnts and be identified as obsolete.
- Keep at least one copy of obsolete documents during the life cycle of the device or according to the applicable regulations.
- Have a documented procedure to identify, archive, protect and restore records.
- Records need to be kept during the life cycle fo the device or acording to the aplicable regulations. 

## Code of Federal Regulations Title 21 Part 820

The organization shall:
- Stablish and keep documented procedures to control all the documents required by Part 820
- Designate individuals to review and approve the docuemnts before submiting them, the approval process shall be documented including date and signature
- Obsolete documents shall be removed from all points of use or prevent its unintended use
- The records shall remain legibles and stored to prevent loss or deterioration
- Keep the records for the expected life cycle of the device, at least two years from comercial distribution
